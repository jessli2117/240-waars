About 

Us



Women Against Abusive Relationships (WAAR) offers empowerment workshops and a weekly podcast covering a variety of topics including but not limited to loving yourself (self-esteem), mental health awareness, sexual assault, teen dating violence, domestic violence, financial health, creativity through the arts and more.







Leadership Committee





Roxanne Epperson

Executive Director/Founder

After surviving an abusive relationship that almost took her life, she became passionately committed to ending violence in the lives of women and girls. She realized that her survival was a blessing from God and believes that she is to use her life-threatening experience to prevent others from suffering the same. To that end, her vision is to bring awareness and education about violence against women and girls to as many people as possible. Her effort toward this endeavor was the founding of Women Against Abusive Relationships (WAAR). WAAR is an awareness, education and prevention program. WAARs mission is to “Break the Cycle” of violence against women and girls and to help them heal. Roxanne is a current or past member of the following organizations that address violence against women issues: Women of Color Network (WOCN) (Past), Women of Color Caucus (WOCC) – a project of the Pennsylvania Coalition Against Domestic Violence (PCADV), Magee-Women’s Hospital of UPMCs Intimate Partner Violence Task Force, and Dionne's Project/Center on Dating and Intimate Partner Violence at Carlow University (Past).



Rhonda L. Fleming

High Advisor

Rhonda L. Fleming is the Education Director at the Women's Center and Shelter (WCS) of Greater Pittsburgh, one of the country’s oldest shelters for women and children. She has served in various prevention roles since her employment there in 1990. As co-author and supervisor of the award-winning curriculum, “Hands Are Not For Hurting®. Rhonda has played a major role in this curriculum being implemented throughout the state of Pennsylvania, Arkansas, Michigan and South Carolina. Most recently Rhonda trained teachers, counselors, advocates and other professionals in Moscow, Russia on teen dating violence prevention. They have now replicated parts of the Hands Are Not For Hurting® curriculum. Rhonda received her Bachelor Degree in Administration of Justice at the University of Pittsburgh and a Masters in Organizational Leadership at Geneva College.
